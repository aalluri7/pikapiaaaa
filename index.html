Tyrant Paradise
Developed a political science 'edutainment' game that the Monterey Public Library currently utilizes as a tool for teaching kids about creating national public policies. Tyrant Paradise is a simulation where the player attempts to keep the nation functioning for as long as possible by maintaining its security, economy, and happiness levels. During the game, a random event happens that usually lowers the value of one of the three, and the player is given a choice of ten randomly chosen policies that they may or may not choose to enact and which would affect any number of resources. Their judgment directly affects the well-being of their nation and determines the success or failure of the game.

         

Drop of Life
Developed a game application called Drop of Life for the Ipod Touch IOS, in which I pitched and designed the mechanism and art assets of the game. The concept of game a simple—a player tries to propel drops of water to reach the top of a flower—and was a test of how well I could prototype a game.

                 

Where You At?
Where you at is a hidden object game designed for pc and the mobile. I designed the game mechanics and modes. Furthermore I worked on all the art assets in the game. In time attack you race against the clock to find as many characters as possible. In find everyone each character you find is removed from the level and your total time is recorded at the end when you find everyone. Two more modes that were designed but not yet implemented were night mode where only the area around your cursor would be illuminated and score mode where you would have a set time to find characters and score multipler for finding people faster.

     

     

Kill Flag
Developed a capture the flag mech simulator called Kill Flag in Unity. After successfully pitching the idea of a mech simulator, I focused on taking the traditional capture the flag game and reinvent the genre. A few changes to the game play were including the concept of nodes that players could capture and spawn from, and implementing a class system so that users each have unique experiences and develop their own unique play styles. The biggest change was taking capture the flag and turning the objective into destroying the flag. Another large contribution to the project was working on the design of weapons, levels, and mechs.

Amphibot
Amphibot was a game created in the 2014 global game jam. It is a puzzle platformer video game where you control two robots with different skill sets to make your way to the end of each level to release poisonous gases to kill all the humans in the level. I worked as a level designer and an artist for this project as well as pitching the original idea.
